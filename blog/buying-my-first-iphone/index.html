Buying My First Iphone

Aug 25 2021

I got my first smartphone at 11 years old. It was a Android, a Sony Xperia Play.

My amazing phone in 2009

I noticed that all of my friends were obsessed with Apple, and this trend never stopped. When I started college, I noticed people wouldn’t invite you to group chats unless you had an Iphone.

I didn’t care for texting, so I never really cared. Also, I loved the quality hardware and full device freedom that Android offered, like right to repair. However, 2 months ago, I switched to the dark side. Thank you Steve Jobs.

After owning an Android my entire life. Here are my thoughts after having an Iphone.

Why I Switched

In 2021, I’ve realized 2 things. The first being that most young people have Iphones. The second, that people seem to see Iphones as a status symbol. Having one means your part of the “cool” kids and not having one means you’re a loser. I’m actually quite confident that most Iphone users couldn’t tell you why they think the Iphone is better than an Android. But that’s a rant for another day.

Anyways, here’s why I think the Iphone is superior to Android. For most cases, it’s the best choice for a phone for young adults.

What I Like About Iphones

There’s 4 things that the Iphone beats Android over

1.) Sending texts through Messenger / Wifi

Sending and receiving messages feels noticeably faster than my Samsung Note 9. Now, I think Google should have fixed this, but sending images and videos is still very seemless.

When I send a video on Android, I’m unsure if it will deliver. And part of that might be my bias from using it before 2018, when SMS was a complete mess. But Apple embeds Tik Toks / Youtube videos, lets you react to certain messages, and it allows group chats without any awkward message timings. Android users have all had the situation when they send a group message and it individually messages it. Apple doesn’t have this.

2.) App Optimizations

Despite having worse hardware, Apple devices outperform Android devices when using popular apps like Snapchat. This is because the businesses optimize their applications for Apple devices. It’s unfortunate, but it means that Apple just wins out. Sending messages or photos via Iphone means other people won’t see quality degradation. This is a good thing.

Also, personally, I love the queue feature that Spotify for Apple devices has, where you can simply slide your finger to queue a song.

3.) Airdrop

You can do some cool tricks via Wifi with Iphones. You can share your wifi password with other users who try to connect by simply pressing a button on your phone. This is faster than needing to spell out the words for your guest out loud. And then there’s Airdrop.

Sharing photos can happen so easily by simply selecting what you want to share, and then having another person request permission. This is great for anytime you’re spending time with friends and have several group photos you want to share with several of different people.

4.) Having people shut up about how great their Iphone Is

Okay this is kind of a joke. I still have to hear Iphone users downtalk Androids. Consumerism is a powerful drug.

Anyways, I originally bought an Iphone to simply be added to more group chats. Since then, I’ve had a couple friends play games with me on the Iphone. I’ve sent some memes to other friends and tik toks embedded through iMessage. I’ve even texted friends via wifi. But I’ve only joined three group chats since having my Iphone. With all of this talk about the Iphone, I will mention a few things that I’ll miss from Android.

What I liked about Android

1.) The feeling of being edgy and on the path less traveled

There’s no greater feeling than this. Having an Iphone means paying more money for a phone with worse hardware. For me, it seems like peak consumerism. It’s spending money simply to spend money. And somehow, this culture has created all of the benefits above.

Going against the herd has it’s downsides, and it means missing out on the features above, and maybe even the respect of certain savants in the Apple cult. But it might be worth it for some.

2.) Knowing I could have complete control over my phone, if I truly wanted

A strong selling point is that an Android device is basically a strong, handheld computer. You can program it do to a variety of tasks, and I’m sure there’s a bunch of ideas and trick people could modify it for.

If you want to customize your phone screen, it’s entirely possible with Android. If you want to download Gameboy or Gamecube emulators, Android’s have the hardware to run them and the user freedom to have them work.

Why the Iphone Is Superior

And it’s for all of these reasons that the Iphone is the superior phone for young adults. If you’re a young professional, I might even say it’s a requirement to have an Iphone. In most scenarios, you won’t need the freedom that Android brings, and instead, you can use the social capital that having an Iphone gives you.

This is still a complicated issue. If everyone were to follow my advice, then I would have to change it. But as it stands in 2021, having an Apple device has benefits.
